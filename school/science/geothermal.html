Geothermals in Iceland

What is geothemal energy?

Geothermal energy is to extrat energy from the heat of the center of the earth. This comes in the form of hot water which is then used to either heat upp cities or spin a turbine to generate electricity. Geothermal energy is regarded as a renewable energy source is because there is so much heat in the core of the earth.

Facts about Iceland

  • Iceland is placed on a tectonic rift and thus is in a volcanicly active area, resulting in ground water getting close enough to magma (not lava click here) that it heats up.
  • Iceland has over 600 hot springs and 200 volcanoes
  • Hydropower provies about 70% of primary energy and the remaining share of geothermal energy is 30%.
  • About 85% of all houses in Iceland are heated with geothermal energy.
